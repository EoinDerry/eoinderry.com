If you've ever Googled a networking concept at midnight, found a wall of text that made no sense, and closed the tab feeling worse than when you started — this site is for you.

I'm Eoin. I've spent years working in IT, managing networks in the real world, and watching people struggle with the gap between what exam prep teaches and what actually happens on the job.

This site exists to close that gap.

## What you'll find here

Every article here is written the way I'd explain something to a colleague across a desk — plain English first, technical detail second. No unnecessary jargon. No assuming you already know the thing I'm supposed to be teaching you.

Topics will cover everything across the CCNA and Network+ syllabi, including:

- Subnetting that actually makes sense
- VLANs, trunking, and switching concepts
- Routing protocols explained from first principles
- Security concepts without the hand-waving
- Real Packet Tracer labs you can download and run yourself

## The Packet Tracer labs

This is the bit I'm most excited about. Every major topic will have a downloadable Packet Tracer lab file — not a screenshot of a topology, an actual working file you can open, break, fix, and learn from.

That's what I always wanted when I was studying. So that's what I'm building.

## Where to start

If you're new, head to the [articles page](/articles/) and pick whatever topic is causing you the most pain right now. That's always the right place to start.

If you prefer video, everything here has a companion video on the [YouTube channel](https://youtube.com/@eoinderry).

Good to have you here.
