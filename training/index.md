Slides don't build engineers. Twenty years of production networks taught
me what actually does: a clear mental model, a lab in front of you, and
an instructor who has seen the failure modes for real. That's what I
deliver — in person or remote, in English, with no death-by-PowerPoint.

## CCNA Training

A complete run through the syllabus for individuals or cohorts, following
the same domain map you'll find on the [roadmap](/roadmap/). Every session
pairs theory with a lab you keep. By the end you can pass the exam — and,
more importantly, you can *do the job* the certification claims you can.

- Full syllabus coverage, weighted like the exam
- Take-home lab topologies and configs for every session
- Exam technique, practice questions and a study plan that survives a full-time job

## Corporate team training

Bespoke deliveries for IT teams — from "our helpdesk needs to stop
escalating everything" to "our sysadmins are inheriting the network".
I'll assess where the team is, build a syllabus around your actual
environment, and leave you with runbooks the team wrote themselves
during the labs.

- Scoped to your stack and your gaps — not a fixed curriculum
- Half-day to multi-week formats, on-site or remote
- Post-engagement support while the new skills bed in

## One-to-one mentoring

For career-changers and early-career engineers who want a straight
answer to "what should I do next?". Regular sessions, a personal study
plan, lab reviews, and interview preparation from someone who has sat
on both sides of the table.

## How an engagement starts

1. **A conversation.** Thirty minutes, no charge, no pitch. You describe
   the goal; I'll tell you honestly whether training is the right tool.
2. **A plan.** Scope, schedule, labs, and a fixed price. No surprises.
3. **The work.** Live sessions, labs, and follow-up until it sticks.
